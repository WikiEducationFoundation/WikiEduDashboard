**Key information about the study:**

- _Informed consent:_ Your informed consent is being sought for research. Participation in the research is voluntary.

- _Purpose of the research:_ The purpose of this study is to learn whether a tool that gives feedback while you edit helps students write better-sourced, encyclopedic content, and to study how students edit. The tool is used as part of the Wikipedia assignment in your course.

- _Expected duration:_ The tool runs while you do your course's Wikipedia editing, for as long as you take part. You do not do any task beyond the editing that your course already asks of you.

- _Procedures._ If you take part, the tool will automatically be installed in your Wikipedia account. While you edit, the tool may show short feedback messages about your editing, such as a reminder to add a citation. You may receive a version of the tool that shows these messages while you edit or a version that does not, and the amount of feedback may differ between versions. The tool records your editing activity, including the text you write and revise, the text you paste, the editing tools you use, and how you respond to any feedback. The tool never changes your text and never prevents you from saving an article; you decide what to write. To evaluate some of the feedback, the tool sends the relevant text to a secure AI service run on a Princeton University account; under our agreement, this text is not used to train the AI. 

- _Withdrawing:_ You may withdraw at any time by uninstalling the tool or by contacting us, after which we collect no further data, with no penalty. Once data has been combined for analysis, it is no longer possible to remove individual records.

- _Risks._ This study involves minimal risk. The editing you do is your normal coursework, and your edits to Wikipedia are public regardless of the study. The main risk is a loss of confidentiality with respect to the editing activity we record, which is not otherwise public. To reduce this risk, we do not collect your name or email, we store the recorded activity on secure servers available only to the research team, we report results only in aggregate, and we never share your individual data with your course instructor.

- _Benefits._ You may not receive a direct benefit from taking part. If you receive a version of the tool that shows feedback, it may help you notice and correct common problems and learn skills such as citing sources and writing in a neutral style, though whether it helps is what the study examines. Your participation will help researchers understand how to use AI to support, rather than replace, student writing.

- _Confidentiality:_ We do not collect your name or email. Your editing activity is stored under your public Wikipedia username and a study identifier, on secure password-protected servers available only to the research team. We will not share your participation or your editing data with your instructor. We publish only aggregate or derived results, and we do not release the recorded editing activity. Aggregate and derived results may be stored indefinitely and used in academic publications, presented at conferences, and shared in scientific data repositories.

- _Compensation:_ There is no compensation for taking part in this study.
